The Studio

Where craft meets the generative stack.

Brain Jelly Labs is the working studio of Youa Vang — visual development artist, creative director, and published author-illustrator with 15+ years across feature animation and television. Credits span Disney Feature Animation, DreamWorks, Warner Bros., Marvel, Hasbro, and Nickelodeon.

The studio develops original content and IP — short films, animated shorts, and original series — using GenAI-native production pipelines. Every project begins with story and traditional visual development: defining the scenes, the beats, the world. From there the pipeline scales through storyboards, image generation, image-to-video, lip-sync, voice, and editorial — all built for sprint-based, platform-ready delivery.

The goal is not to replace craft. It's to build a production system where a single artist can develop, produce, and ship original IP at a pace that was previously impossible.

Philosophy

How the Studio Works

01
Story First. Always.

Every project starts with a story — a moment, an emotion, a question worth asking. From there the work moves into traditional visual development: defining the scenes, breaking the beats, establishing the world before a single tool is opened.

02
Identity Before Tools

Every project goes through full visual development — expression sheets, orthographic drawings, color keys, personality documents. The AI tools scale what's already locked. They don't define it.

03
Pipeline as Creative Infrastructure

Each IP is built with a documented production system: reusable shot plates, defined camera language, consistent voice direction, and editorial templates. Sprint-based cycles from brief to published content.

04
Consistency at Scale

Visual identity must hold across every frame. Character sheets start traditionally — thumbnail sketches and drawings — then are finished with AI help and verified by hand for scale, placement, cloth, and proportion before becoming production assets. Locked at source, not fixed in post.

05
Built to Teach

Every production decision is documented. As faculty at ArtCenter College of Design, the studio's work directly informs how the next generation of artists learns to operate inside AI-native workflows — building systems that are rigorous, repeatable, and rooted in the craft principles that have always made animation worth watching.
